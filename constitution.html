Constitution

The operating principles, membership expectations, and elected leadership structure of the FSU Mathematics Graduate Student Council.

Living document

This constitution is updated regularly. Last updated August 2024

Article 01

Purpose & activity

Activities of the Council

The council meets, gathers graduate student perspectives, supports incoming students, and assists with major departmental activities.

  1. 01

    Meet every two weeks, or more frequently when necessary, to discuss graduate student concerns and concerns or requests communicated by the administration.

  2. 02

    Survey the broader mathematics graduate student population to identify comments or concerns that should be addressed by the administration.

  3. 03

    Help incoming graduate students with transportation and accommodations.

  4. 04

    Meet with prospective graduate students to discuss FSU Mathematics graduate programs and graduate student life at FSU more generally.

  5. 05

    Assist with Graduate Student Research Week and the Graduate Student Picnic each Spring, as well as Orientation Week in the Fall, either by volunteering directly or by encouraging broader graduate student participation.

Article 02

Eligibility

Joining the Council

New members are added as graduating or departing members leave the council. Membership should seek balanced representation across departmental areas and should also strive for diversity in gender expression, nationality, race, and other backgrounds.

Membership is open to any Mathematics graduate student who has:

01

Nomination

Received a nomination from a current GSC member during the second-to-last meeting of the semester.

02

Qualifying exams

Passed all Qualifying exams required for their departmental area.

03

Council approval

Received a unanimous approval vote from the current members of the council.

Article 03

Active service

Maintaining Membership

GSC members are expected to remain active in meetings, council activities, and council communication. The following circumstances may be grounds for removal from the council.

02

Meeting attendance

Missing two scheduled meetings in one semester without providing a reasonable excuse, such as a teaching conflict or medical emergency.

@

Communication

Failing to respond to email correspondence on two occasions, including requests such as surveys used to plan meetings.

∅

Departmental participation

Never or rarely volunteering during a semester for departmental activities supported by the council.

Article 04

Leadership

Elected Positions

The council has four elected positions with specific responsibilities: two co-chairs, one secretary, and one social media manager.

01

Two positions

Co-Chairs

Full academic-year term

Co-chairs are elected during the second-to-last meeting of the semester in alternating semesters. Each co-chair serves for a full academic year, and the two co-chairs do not begin their terms in the same semester.

  • Lead council meetings.
  • Consolidate information from meetings for presentation to the administration.
  • Communicate administration needs to the rest of the council.
02

One position

Secretary

Elected each Spring · One-year term
  • Take minutes during meetings and email the minutes to members after meetings.
  • Maintain records of meeting and activity attendance.
  • Create and distribute flyers when needed.
03

One position

Social Media Manager

Elected each Fall · One-year term
  • Maintain the GSC website and social media page with current member information, updating them monthly together with the meeting timeline.
  • Expand GSC social media when appropriate, for example by adding videos that answer common graduate school questions.
Article 05

Election procedure

Voting for Executive Positions

2+

Multiple nominations

When two or more nominees are seeking the same position, voting is conducted online. The vote is anonymous to everyone except executive members who are not themselves standing for election.

1

Single nomination

When only one person is nominated for a position, voting is conducted in person.

Objection procedure

Anyone wishing to object to the nominated person should send an email that includes an alternate nominee.
